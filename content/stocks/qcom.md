Qualcomm's franchise extends beyond mobile processors into automotive, IoT, and increasingly, edge AI processing. The company's recent 6% surge reflects growing recognition of its AI PC processor opportunity and recovery in smartphone demand.

The buy zone at $218-226 represents a healthy retest following the recent surge. Avoid chasing the momentum—let the stock consolidate and provide a favorable entry point. The zone aligns with prior resistance turning to support, a classic technical pattern.

The Snapdragon X Elite and Plus processors for Windows PCs represent Qualcomm's most significant diversification opportunity in years. Microsoft's push for AI PCs with neural processing units (NPUs) plays directly into QCOM's strengths. Initial OEM adoption from Dell, HP, and Lenovo validates the product positioning.

Smartphone recovery is underway, particularly in China where replacement cycles are accelerating. Qualcomm's Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 captured flagship design wins across all major Android OEMs. The mix shift toward premium devices benefits both ASPs and margins.

Automotive remains a long-term growth driver with $30+ billion in design win backlog. The Snapdragon Digital Chassis spans infotainment, ADAS, and digital cockpit applications. Revenue recognition lags design wins by 2-3 years, providing visibility into mid-decade growth.

The licensing business stabilized after resolving disputes with Apple and other OEMs. While not a growth driver, it generates high-margin recurring revenue that supports R&D investment and shareholder returns.

Stop placement below $213 protects against failed consolidation or broader market weakness. The targets of $235, $242, and $250 reflect resistance levels from prior trading ranges and measured technical moves.

Catalysts include AI PC adoption metrics, smartphone market share data from China, and automotive revenue inflection signals. Watch for any indication of Apple developing in-house modem technology—while timeline remains uncertain, it represents a key long-term risk.

The 2.4:1 risk-reward ratio coupled with multiple growth vectors justifies the A grade. QCOM offers diversified exposure to AI edge computing, mobile recovery, and automotive electrification.
