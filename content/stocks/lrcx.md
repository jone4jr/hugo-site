Lam Research specializes in wafer etch and deposition equipment, with particular strength in NAND flash memory manufacturing. The company's technological moat in multi-patterning etch makes it indispensable for advanced node production.

The $387-396 retest zone provides a technical entry point after recent consolidation. LRCX has shown propensity to base at these levels before resuming uptrends. The zone sits above key moving averages, suggesting the intermediate trend remains intact.

NAND flash is recovering from a severe downcycle as supply discipline improves and AI-driven demand for storage accelerates. Enterprise SSDs for AI servers require dramatically more capacity than traditional servers, driving NAND bit demand growth even as PC and smartphone markets remain weak.

DRAM equipment sales benefit from HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) adoption in AI accelerators. HBM manufacturing requires additional process steps versus commodity DRAM, increasing equipment intensity. Lam's etch technology is critical for through-silicon vias in HBM stacks.

Logic and foundry customers are transitioning to GAA architectures that require more complex etch steps. Lam's Flex product line addresses multi-patterning requirements at 3nm and below nodes. Equipment sales per wafer increase as process complexity rises.

Customer support services generate recurring revenue from the installed base. Etch chambers require frequent maintenance and consumables replacement, creating high-margin aftermarket business that smooths cyclical volatility.

The $379 stop level sits below key support and moving average clusters. Breaking this area would signal technical weakness or broader semiconductor equipment sector pressure. Targets of $405, $418, and $430 represent measured moves and prior resistance levels.

Risks include memory market recovery timing, competitive pressure from Applied Materials in overlapping markets, and customer concentration in Samsung and SK Hynix. Monitor HBM production ramp signals and NAND industry discipline.

The 2.1:1 risk-reward with memory recovery exposure justifies the A- grade. LRCX offers pure-play memory equipment exposure with technology leadership in etch processes.
