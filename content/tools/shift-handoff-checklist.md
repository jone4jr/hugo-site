Many operational surprises begin in the gap between shifts.

Information gets assumed instead of transferred. Open issues stay in someone's head instead of making it to the next team. Then the incoming shift starts behind, recreates work, or misses a developing problem that someone thought they had already communicated.

This checklist gives leaders a simple structure for handoffs that are short enough to use and specific enough to matter.
