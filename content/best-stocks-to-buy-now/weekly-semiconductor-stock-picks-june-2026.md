## Best risk-adjusted focus

The cleanest focus list is NVDA, AVGO, AMD, and QCOM. Those four names offer the best mix of liquidity, theme alignment, relative strength, and defined risk zones. MRVL and TER can work, but they need smaller sizing and cleaner intraday consolidation because the volatility is higher.

## NVDA: Best balance of liquidity, sector alignment, and controlled risk

NVDA is the top-ranked candidate because it gives the best blend of liquidity, AI leadership, semiconductor strength, and manageable entry structure. The stock closed near $210.69, up about 2.85%, with very high volume. Its 20-day and 50-day moving-average area was roughly $211.79 and $209.31, which puts the setup near a logical retest zone rather than a pure chase.

The clean entry is not a gap-up buy. NVDA is strongest as a watchlist candidate if SMH holds strength and the stock holds the $206-$211 zone. A failure below $203 or failed SMH support weakens the setup.

## AVGO: Strong rebound, but not as clean as NVDA

AVGO closed at $411.35, up 4.7%, with volume around 43.9 million shares, well above its 50-day average of about 26 million. That volume acceleration is constructive because it shows real participation behind the move.

The issue is entry quality. AVGO is still below its recent 52-week high, and the better setup is a controlled pullback or breakout retest instead of chasing strength. The key zone is $401-$412, with a risk line around $397 if it loses the 50-day moving-average reclaim.

## AMD: High upside with higher valuation and volatility risk

AMD closed around $537.37, up about 4.7%. Its 50-day moving average was cited near $508.85 and the 200-day near $481.88, so the larger trend structure is still favorable.

The tradeoff is extension. AMD already sits above key moving averages, which raises chase risk. The cleaner setup is a pullback, flag, or 1-hour reset into the $525-$538 area. A gap-up without consolidation is lower quality for capital protection.

## QCOM: Strong short-term relative strength and volume surge

QCOM rose about 6.17% to $226.11, with volume around 45.9 million shares, more than double its 50-day average of 22.6 million. That type of volume surge can point to accumulation, especially when it happens inside a stronger sector tape.

The one-day move also creates chase risk. The better plan is to watch the $218-$226 area and see whether it holds as support. QCOM is best treated as a retest trade, with $213 as the invalidation level from this screen.

## MRVL and TER: Higher-beta momentum trades

MRVL and TER both sit in the higher-beta portion of the list. MRVL posted a strong latest-session gain with unusually high volume, while TER has an attractive consolidation zone in the $421-$438 range. Both require smaller sizing and cleaner intraday structure because the stops must be wider.

MRVL is more attractive after a 1-hour or 4-hour consolidation that tightens the stop. TER needs to hold $414 to keep the setup intact.

## AMAT and LRCX: Equipment exposure with cleaner retest areas

AMAT and LRCX bring semiconductor equipment exposure into the list. AMAT has a support retest zone around $612-$622, while LRCX has a $387-$396 retest zone. Both are less aggressive than the highest-beta momentum names but still depend on the broader chip tape holding together.

## AEIS and ALAB: Emerging opportunities with tighter discipline

AEIS passes as an emerging opportunity because it has AI and data-center exposure and recently regained a key level. It does not qualify as a core large-cap trade in the same way as NVDA or AVGO because institutional behavior was described as mixed to negative.

ALAB has strong momentum, but it is also one of the highest-risk names on the list after an 11%+ move. It only qualifies on a pullback into the $384-$405 area. Buying ALAB extended violates the capital-protection discipline behind this screen.

## Names that did not make the actionable list

CRWV stays watchlist-only for this 5-14 day system. It has AI infrastructure exposure and liquidity, but negative EPS, elevated volatility, and weaker risk control keep it outside the actionable list.

Financials and healthcare are also lower priority for this specific swing screen because XLF and XLV underperformed SPY on the latest available data. Under this model, that violates the sector-strength requirement for fresh long ideas.

## Execution rules for this week

The cleanest action is not to buy gaps. Use pullbacks, breakout retests, or 1-hour and 4-hour consolidations. Keep total AI and semiconductor theme concentration below 40% of capital assigned to this strategy.

Micron earnings on June 24 can move the entire chip tape, so position sizing should be reduced before that event or paired with tighter, pre-defined invalidation levels.

Final ranking: NVDA, AVGO, AMD, QCOM, MRVL, TER, AMAT, LRCX, AEIS, ALAB.
