### What You'll Learn

**The 14 Principles of W. Edwards Deming**

**Principle 1: Create Constancy of Purpose**
- Long-term commitment to improvement
- Beyond short-term profits
- Innovation and research
- Continuous improvement of products and services
- Planning for the future

**Principle 2: Adopt the New Philosophy**
- Reject defects and negative attitudes
- Management must lead transformation
- Creating quality culture
- Zero tolerance for poor quality

**Principle 3: Cease Dependence on Inspection**
- Build quality in, don't inspect it in
- Statistical evidence of built-in quality
- Prevent defects rather than detect them
- Cost of poor quality understanding

**Principle 4: End Lowest Price Supplier Practice**
- Total cost, not purchase price
- Single supplier relationships
- Long-term partnerships
- Supplier development

**Principle 5: Improve Constantly and Forever**
- Continuous improvement of all systems
- PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle
- Never-ending improvement journey
- Innovation and optimization

**Principle 6: Institute Training on the Job**
- Structured training programs
- Developing capability
- Investment in people
- Standardized training methods

**Principle 7: Institute Leadership**
- Leaders as coaches, not supervisors
- Removing barriers to pride of workmanship
- Managing systems, not people
- Leadership vs management

**Principle 8: Drive Out Fear**
- Creating psychological safety
- Encouraging questions and innovation
- Fear inhibits improvement
- Building trust

**Principle 9: Break Down Barriers Between Departments**
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Systems thinking
- Internal customer concept
- Eliminating silos

**Principle 10: Eliminate Slogans and Targets**
- Focus on methods, not exhortations
- Management must provide system improvements
- Arbitrary targets create wrong behaviors
- Data-driven goals

**Principle 11: Eliminate Numerical Quotas and Management by Objectives**
- Quotas encourage wrong behavior
- Focus on quality, not quantity
- Understanding variation
- Capability-based goals

**Principle 12: Remove Barriers to Pride of Workmanship**
- Allow people to do their best work
- Eliminate annual ratings
- Focus on intrinsic motivation
- Respect for employees

**Principle 13: Institute Vigorous Program of Education and Self-Improvement**
- Developing people broadly
- Continuous learning culture
- Personal development
- Growing capability

**Principle 14: Put Everybody to Work on Transformation**
- Transformation is everyone's job
- Management commitment essential
- Creating structure for change
- Overcoming resistance

### Who Was W. Edwards Deming?

- American statistician, engineer, and management consultant
- Taught Japanese industry quality methods post-WWII
- Deming Prize named in his honor (Japan's highest quality award)
- Influenced Toyota Production System development
- Revolutionized quality management thinking
- Author of "Out of the Crisis" and other seminal works
