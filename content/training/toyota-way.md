### What You'll Learn

**The 14 Principles of The Toyota Way**

**Section 1: Long-Term Philosophy**
- Principle 1: Base decisions on long-term philosophy, even at the expense of short-term financial goals

**Section 2: The Right Process Will Produce the Right Results**
- Principle 2: Create continuous process flow to bring problems to the surface
- Principle 3: Use pull systems to avoid overproduction
- Principle 4: Level out the workload (Heijunka)
- Principle 5: Build a culture of stopping to fix problems (Jidoka)
- Principle 6: Standardized tasks for continuous improvement
- Principle 7: Use visual controls so no problems are hidden
- Principle 8: Use only reliable, thoroughly tested technology

**Section 3: Add Value by Developing Your People and Partners**
- Principle 9: Grow leaders who live the philosophy
- Principle 10: Develop exceptional people and teams
- Principle 11: Respect extended network of partners and suppliers

**Section 4: Solving Root Problems Drives Organizational Learning**
- Principle 12: Go see for yourself (Genchi Genbutsu)
- Principle 13: Make decisions slowly by consensus, implement rapidly
- Principle 14: Become a learning organization through relentless reflection (Hansei)

### Core Toyota Concepts

**Respect for People**
- Developing people, not just using them
- Trusting team members
- Challenge and empowerment
- Teaching vs. telling

**Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)**
- Daily incremental improvement
- Scientific problem-solving
- PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle
- Employee engagement in improvement

**Just-In-Time (JIT)**
- Takt time and flow
- Pull systems and Kanban
- Reducing inventory
- Revealing problems

**Jidoka (Built-In Quality)**
- Stop and notify of abnormalities
- Separate human work from machine work
- Line-stop authority (Andon)
- Error-proofing (Poka-Yoke)

**Genchi Genbutsu (Go and See)**
- Go to the source
- Understand deeply
- Make decisions based on firsthand knowledge
- Verify data personally
