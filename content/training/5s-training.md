## 5S Training - Foundation for Lean Management

Master the foundational workplace organization methodology that underpins all lean manufacturing efforts. This course teaches not just the 5S steps, but how 5S creates the conditions for continuous improvement and lean management.

### What You'll Learn

**The 5S System**

**1. Sort (Seiri)**
- Distinguishing needed from unneeded items
- Red tag strategy
- Criteria for keeping vs removing
- Dealing with "just in case" thinking

**2. Set in Order (Seiton)**
- A place for everything, everything in its place
- Visual location indicators
- Shadow boards and floor marking
- Point-of-use storage
- Ergonomic considerations

**3. Shine (Seiso)**
- Cleaning as inspection
- Identifying abnormalities
- Cleaning standards
- Equipment care basics
- Daily cleaning routines

**4. Standardize (Seiketsu)**
- Creating visual standards
- Standard work for 5S maintenance
- Checklists and audits
- Sustaining improvements
- Making standards visual

**5. Sustain (Shitsuke)**
- Building habits and discipline
- Management role in sustainability
- Auditing systems
- Recognition and accountability
- Continuous improvement of standards

### The Critical Link: 5S and Lean Management

This course uniquely emphasizes how 5S enables lean management:

**5S Creates Visual Management**
- Problems become immediately visible
- Abnormalities are obvious
- Standards are clear
- Deviation triggers response

**5S Enables Standard Work**
- Tools and materials in consistent locations
- Predictable work environment
- Reduced search time
- Foundation for timing studies

**5S Supports Quality**
- Cleanliness prevents contamination
- Organization prevents errors
- Shine reveals equipment problems
- Standards ensure consistency

**5S Facilitates Flow**
- Eliminates motion waste
- Reduces changeover time
- Enables one-piece flow
- Minimizes work-in-process

**5S Builds Improvement Culture**
- Employee ownership of workspace
- Daily attention to small problems
- Practice of PDCA cycle
- Foundation for Kaizen

**5S Reveals True Problems**
- Excess inventory becomes visible
- Material flow issues surface
- Equipment problems are exposed
- Process inefficiencies are clear
