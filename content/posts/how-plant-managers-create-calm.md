Plant managers do not create calm by acting relaxed while everything burns. They create calm by making the work more legible.

People feel calmer when they know what matters, what is changing, what is staying the same, who owns the next move, and when the next decision will happen.

## Reduce priority noise

Too many urgent priorities create panic even when everyone is working hard.

A plant manager creates calm by naming the real priority and explaining the tradeoffs. That does not mean other work stops. It means the team understands what leads the day.

## Make decisions visible

Unclear decisions create rumor. Rumor creates motion without alignment.

When a decision is made, say what was decided, why, who owns the next step, and when it will be reviewed.

## Stay consistent under pressure

Teams watch leaders most closely when the day is bad. If leaders abandon the standards under pressure, the standards were never really standards.

Consistency is not rigidity. It is the habit of protecting the most important behaviors when pressure rises.

## Close the loop

Calm grows when people see unresolved issues move somewhere useful.

A closed loop tells the team: leadership saw it, decided something, and came back. That pattern lowers the emotional temperature more than any slogan.
